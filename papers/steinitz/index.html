A contragredient view of conformal placement and Steinitz balancing

The exact orbit identity, its balanced-permutation primal, and herding as the relaxation. Rough draft, not for dissemination.

The exact identity

Conformal validity is an orbit-averaging identity for the permutation representation, stated in contragredient form through the Reynolds projector: \(\langle P^{\vee}f, v\rangle = \langle f, Pv\rangle\). Average the evaluation functional over placements, or average the placement vector holding the functional fixed; the counting identity makes the orbit average exactly \(k/(n+1)\), and exchangeability makes the one designated test placement an even draw on the orbit.

Steinitz balancing, and herding as its relaxation

The primal construction under the same permutation action is Steinitz balancing: order a zero-sum population so every prefix discrepancy stays bounded. Harvey and Samadi posed herding over a finite population as exactly this permutation problem; generic herding is the with-replacement relaxation. Conformal placements are themselves a scalar Steinitz population, and the note gives an explicit balanced ordering whose running coverage is within \(1/(2t)\) of nominal at every prefix, exact at the full orbit. On the function–measure pairing, the literal transpose of calibration is balanced sampling and exact cubature (the cube method; Tchakaloff), for which herding is one iterative solver. None of this removes exchangeability from one-shot conformal prediction.
