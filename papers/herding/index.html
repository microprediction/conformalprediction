Technical note

Moving the slots, not the balls

Conformal prediction as the contragredient of herding.

Read the PDF LaTeX source

The idea

Herding (Welling 2009) moves the balls: it steers particles \(x_t\) so their empirical feature averages balance a fixed target, \(\tfrac1n\sum_t \phi(x_t)\approx\mu\), and a bounded weight sequence buys the quasi–Monte Carlo rate \(O(1/n)\) where i.i.d.\ sampling gets \(O(1/\sqrt n)\). Split conformal's coverage proof is the dual operation: the realized scores are never touched, and what moves is the designation “this slot holds the test point.” Because the quantile threshold is a symmetric function of the scores, a deterministic counting identity holds — exactly \(\lceil(1-\alpha)(n+1)\rceil\) of the \(n+1\) placements of that label point at covered scores, for any score vector. Herding holds the roulette wheel fixed and moves the balls; conformal holds the balls fixed and rotates the slot labels under the pointer.

The contragredient, and the 1/n ledger

The duality is stated on the permutation pairing: rearrange the data with \(P_\pi\), or hold the data fixed and pull the coordinate functional back, \(e_{n+1}^{*}\mapsto e_{\pi(n+1)}^{*}\) — the contragredient side. The payoff is a clean reading of the Barber–Pananjady time-series bound: deleting \(\tau\) slots damages the rank count by at most \(\tau\) (order statistics are insertion/deletion stable), worth \(\tau/n\) of coverage, and the only probabilistic charge is the total-variation failure of rotation invariance, \(\bar\Psi_\tau\le 2\beta(\tau)\). Nothing concentrates, so no square root appears — the same reason herding beats Monte Carlo. Bounded accumulated error, divided by \(n\): herding's bounded feature discrepancy, conformal's bounded count of damaged ranks.

What is and is not claimed

The duality is exact at the level of the finite-sample ledger and silent on the statistics: the step from “\(k\) of \(n{+}1\) placements are covered” to a probability is exchangeability, or its mixing surrogate, and slot bookkeeping does not supply it. Conformal also does no active steering — the balance herding must manufacture is already present in the ranks. And moving labels over fixed scores creates no information: it re-levels coverage while the conditional sharpness stays with the scores, the information-gap point in another costume.

The companion demo runs both mechanisms side by side: kernel herding closing on its target at \(1/n\) against Monte Carlo's \(1/\sqrt n\), and the slot pointer rotating over a fixed, arbitrarily dependent score vector while the covered count sits at exactly \(k\) of \(n+1\).
