Technical note

Herding as the contragredient of conformal prediction

The contragredient of a group action is the same action carried by the functionals instead of the points. Herding is conformal calibration with the action moved to the primal side — and conformal came first.

The result

Both mechanisms control one argument of the pairing \(\langle h,\nu\rangle=\int h\,d\nu\) while a cumulative discrepancy stays bounded by a constant: herding constructs the measure against fixed tests (\(\|w_n\|\) bounded), conformal constructs the acceptance test against the fixed empirical measure of scores (\(\sum_i(\mathbf 1\{S_i\le q_n\}-p)\in[p,1+p)\)), and its validity proof transports the evaluation functional across the sample — exactly \(\lceil(1-\alpha)(n+1)\rceil\) of the \(n+1\) placements are covered, for any scores, with exchangeability entering only to make the realized placement an even draw.

Arguments cross the duality, both ways

The relation is useful exactly insofar as proofs cross it, and the note crosses it in both directions through a stability lemma for the pairing (perturbations of either argument cost linearly; nothing concentrates). Toward conformal: the Barber–Pananjady coverage floor for split conformal on \(\beta\)-mixing series, stated exactly with the switch coefficient defined, factors into the lemma’s two legs. Buffer deletion is a measure perturbation costing \(\tau/(n+1)\); the switch coefficient is a distributional total variation; the linearity of their rate is the linearity of the pairing, and blocking arguments pay \(\sqrt{\tau/n}\) because they leave it. Toward herding: a low-discrepancy calibration design (quantile grid, herding, kernel thinning) makes realized coverage deterministic to \(D+1/n\), collapsing its dispersion from the sampling order \(n^{-1/2}\) to the herding order \(n^{-1}\), at the stated price of trading the exchangeability symmetry for determinism against the marginal law.

Scope

The duality is exact at the level of the finite-sample ledger and silent on the statistics: the step from “\(k\) of \(n{+}1\) placements are covered” to a probability is exchangeability, or its mixing surrogate, and slot bookkeeping does not supply it. And dual-space steering creates no information: thresholds and labels moving over fixed scores re-level coverage exactly, while the conditional sharpness stays with the scores — the information-gap point in another costume.

The companion demo runs both mechanisms side by side: kernel herding closing on its target at \(1/n\) against Monte Carlo's \(1/\sqrt n\), and the slot pointer rotating over a fixed, arbitrarily dependent score vector while the covered count sits at exactly \(k\) of \(n+1\).
