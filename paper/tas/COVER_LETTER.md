# Cover letter — The American Statistician

Dear Editor,

I am submitting **"Marginally Useful: Formalizing the Information Gap in Conformal
Prediction"** for consideration as a general article in *The American Statistician*.

Conformal prediction has become a standard recommendation for "adding uncertainty" to a
forecast, and its finite-sample, distribution-free coverage guarantee is routinely read as
evidence that the forecast underneath is good. These are different things, and the paper
makes the distinction exact. For a fixed location predictor whose residuals are re-leveled
by a single shared shape — the setting in which conformal prediction is used to emit a
predictive distribution — I show that the expected logarithmic-score shortfall relative to
the conditional oracle equals exactly one quantity: the mutual information between the
residual and the input. Conformalization re-levels coverage but cannot reduce this quantity;
closing it requires modeling the conditional residual, which the conformal step does not do.
The identity yields a simple two-coordinate diagnostic on which conformalization moves a
report toward exact coverage but never toward a better proper score.

I believe this fits *The American Statistician* because it is a clarification, with one exact
result and a usable diagnostic, of a method now in very wide use — and because it is precise,
rather than dismissive, about where conformal prediction is the right tool: when a coverage
certificate is itself the deliverable (selective prediction, risk control, conformal
*p*-values), the method is not merely sound but often ideal. The paper is explicit about
provenance: the impossibility of distribution-free conditional coverage, the conformal
predictive system, and the calibration/sharpness decomposition are prior work, cited as
such; the contribution is their combination into the exact identity, five equivalent
readings of it, and the resulting diagnostic.

The manuscript is not under consideration at any other journal, has not been published
previously, and has a single author. An interactive companion and the reproduction code
(including a script that verifies the central identity numerically to machine precision) are
publicly available at https://conformalprediction.net; the figures and benchmark are
generated by the included scripts.

Thank you for considering the manuscript.
