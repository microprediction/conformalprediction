Other Sites & Blogs

Places that write about conformal prediction, from enthusiasts to skeptics. A partial list; additions welcome by issue or PR.

  • arg min (Ben Recht) — the sharpest of the skeptics; the 2024 series (Cover Songs, From Intervals to Bands) asks what the guarantee actually buys.
  • Mindful Modeler (Christoph Molnar) — a long-running series that became the book Introduction to Conformal Prediction with Python.
  • A Gentle Introduction to Conformal Prediction (Anastasios Angelopoulos & Stephen Bates) — hosted on arXiv but read like a living tutorial, with code; the on-ramp for much of the modern wave.
  • Thierry Moudiki — frequent, practical posts on conformal prediction and probabilistic forecasting, usually with runnable code.
  • Statistical Modeling (Jessica Hullman, writing on Gelman’s blog) — loving, hating, and sometimes misinterpreting conformal prediction for medical decisions.
  • Normal Deviate (Larry Wasserman) — dormant, but the 2013 post on assumption-free inference remains a clear early statement of what conformal does and does not need.
  • Cosma Shalizi’s notebooks — a running annotated bibliography on conformal prediction, in his usual encyclopedic style.
  • Daniel Bethell — an end-to-end tutorial from the basic recipe through cross-conformal and conformalized quantile regression, heavy on code and pictures.
  • tidymodels (Max Kuhn and the Posit team) — conformal regression intervals for the R crowd, with a clear walkthrough of the reference error distribution.
  • Cyrus Samii — a compact worked tutorial following Lei et al., with illustrations; Shalizi’s strong recommendation.
  • Awesome Conformal Prediction (Valeriy Manokhin) — the field’s largest curated link list, alongside his Medium posts.
  • alrw.net (Vovk et al.) — not a blog: the working-paper series behind Algorithmic Learning in a Random World, straight from the source.
