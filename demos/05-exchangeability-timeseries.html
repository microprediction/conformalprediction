Demo 05

Exchangeability & time series

The one true guarantee rests on a single assumption. Time series quietly violate it.

Every coverage statement in conformal prediction is paid for by one assumption: the calibration data and the future point are exchangeable — their joint distribution is invariant to reordering. That is plausible for an i.i.d. sample. It is rarely true for a series unfolding in time, where the level trends and occasionally jumps to a new regime. Drop exchangeability and the finite-sample guarantee does not weaken gracefully; it simply stops holding.

Below we forecast a nonstationary series \(y_t = \text{level}_t + \varepsilon_t\) with a trivial online predictor \(\hat y_t\) (a rolling mean). We calibrate split conformal once on \([0, t_0]\) — the dashed line — taking the band half-width \(q = \lceil (n+1)(1-\alpha)\rceil\)-th smallest of \(|y-\hat y|\), and then freeze it: \(\hat y_t \pm q\) for all \(t>t_0\). Turn up the drift slider and watch the green points go red.

The standard repair is Adaptive Conformal Inference (Gibbs & Candès, 2021): treat the miscoverage level itself as something to track. Maintain \(\alpha_t\), build the band from the \((1-\alpha_t)\) empirical residual quantile, observe whether you missed, and update $$\alpha_{t+1} = \alpha_t + \gamma\,(\alpha - \mathrm{err}_t), \qquad \mathrm{err}_t = \mathbf 1\{y_t \notin C_t\}.$$ Miss too often and \(\alpha_t\) shrinks, widening the next band; over-cover and it grows. This is a control loop, not a guarantee. What it buys you is a theorem about the long-run time average of \(\mathrm{err}_t\) converging to \(\alpha\). Toggle the ACI band on and compare.

Above: rolling coverage in a trailing 40-step window. The fixed band (blue) sits near target while the data are roughly stationary, then collapses once the trend and the regime jump bite. ACI’s trailing coverage (orange) oscillates around target — sometimes well above, sometimes well below — while its cumulative average (purple, dashed) crawls toward \(1-\alpha\). The average is fine. The window you are standing in is not.

Takeaway. Exchangeability is the load-bearing wall. Knock it out and split conformal’s finite-sample coverage evaporates out-of-sample. ACI restores only a long-run average miscoverage rate — a far weaker, more diffuse object than the original statement, and one that says nothing about the step in front of you. For forecasting, where you want a sharp predictive distribution now, “correct on average over all time” is close to the worst guarantee you could be handed. Demo 06 asks whether plain probabilistic recalibration would have served you better all along.
